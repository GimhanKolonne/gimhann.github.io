BENTOTA TURTLE HATCHERY

WELCOME

The Bentota Turtle Hatchery Project is a popular tourist attraction in Bentota. The pristine golden shores that entice you to our island also serve as an enticing nesting site for several endangered turtle species, ranging from the Olive Ridley to the Leatherback turtle. The project's goal is to conserve and protect the eggs from predators and other threats. Visit the turtle hatchery in Bentota to learn more about these endangered sea creatures.

Sri Lanka is one of the world's major biodiversity hotspots, and wildlife conservation is a top priority for the island. Turtle hatcheries dotted along Sri Lanka's western coast address the issue by investing in the survival of the island's turtles.

When the eggs arrive at the hatchery, they are safely buried in sand until they emerge from their shells, at which point they are moved and placed in tanks for the first few days of their lives.

At sunset, you can see the baby turtles being released on the beach and following them back to the ocean.
